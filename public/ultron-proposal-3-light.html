Ultron Proposal 3
When context loss kills scaling
When you're scaling from 3 to 10 people, context loss is inevitable. Here's the fix.
At 3 people: Everything is known
Everyone knows everything. You talk. Things happen. Context is in the air.
At 7 people: Context starts slipping
Who knows the exact customer pain we're solving? What's our north star metric? Why did we say no to that feature? What's our playbook?
Context starts dying.
At 10 people: Context is completely gone
New hire doesn't know founding decision logic. Team doesn't remember tech stack choice. Customer success doesn't understand positioning nuance. Sales doesn't know financial constraints.
Everyone's working from different versions of the truth.
That's when scaling breaks.
Not growth. Not market fit.
Context loss breaks scaling.
Ultron solves this with one principle:
Context is infrastructure.
Not just your knowledge. Not in your head. Your business rules stored, documented, accessible everywhere.
New hire onboards in hours instead of weeks.
Sales uses your positioning framework instead of improvising.
Operations runs from your playbook instead of guessing.
Customer success answers questions aligned with your strategy.
Everyone's thinking with your business brain.
