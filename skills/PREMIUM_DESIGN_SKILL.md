# PREMIUM VISUAL DESIGN SKILL

**Role:** Premium Visual Design Director  
**Focus:** Surface quality, materiality, finish, polish, tonal control  
**Output:** Expensive, controlled, intentional visual assets

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Premium ≠ More Effects
Premium means:
- Fewer effects, better controlled
- Subtle integration
- Less obvious application
- Maximum intention

**Rule:** Everything must feel deliberate. Nothing accidental.

### 2. Design Objective
Communicate:
- Control
- Intention
- Clarity
- Weight
- Restraint
- Calm visual tension

Feel:
- Designed, not assembled
- Expensive, not decorated
- Clean, not empty
- Refined, not overworked

## Surfaces & Materiality

### Dark Surface Construction
Build with:
- Slight tonal lift at top
- Subtle internal gradient (1-4% variation)
- Faint edge definition (Slate Light border, 1px, 20% opacity)
- Restrained highlight
- Softened shadow integration

Feel: Dense, cinematic, grounded, calm, premium

### Light Surface Construction
Build with:
- Gentle warm tonal variation
- Soft internal depth
- Faint border separation
- Mild background contrast
- Subtle shadow anchoring

Feel: Soft, calm, intentional, editorial, breathable

### Rules for All Surfaces
- Never feel flat or empty
- Create atmosphere without performance
- Support foreground without competing
- Remove visible effort

---

## Gradient System

### Premium Gradient Rules
- Keep tonal difference small (4-8% better than 25-30%)
- Prefer soft transitions, never harsh
- Combine linear + radial only if unified
- Use to shape surfaces, not aggressively color them

### Appropriate Uses
- Background surfaces
- Dark panels
- Accent surfaces
- Soft highlights
- Restrained glow zones

### What to Avoid
- Visible banding
- Aggressive transitions
- Cheap brand-gradient behavior
- Fake glossy surfaces

---

## Shadow System

### Premium Shadow Rules
- Large, soft, low-opacity
- Fewer shadows, but better
- Layer depth when needed
- Tint slightly for warmth on neutral palettes
- On dark surfaces, even more restrained

### Shadow Purpose
- Makes objects feel placed
- Gives surfaces weight
- Helps layers read naturally
- Separation, not drama

### Avoid
- Small, hard shadows
- Low blur values
- High opacity
- Default drop shadows everywhere
- Software-style templates

---

## Border System

### Border Rules
- Extremely subtle
- Low contrast
- Sometimes nearly invisible
- Functional separation, never decoration
- On dark panels, borders should barely be felt

### Purpose
- Clarify object edges
- Define panels without boxing
- Work with shadow and highlight

### Avoid
- Bright white borders
- Thick outlines
- High-contrast strokes
- Boxed-in cards
- Ornamental borders

---

## Highlights & Light Behavior

### Premium Highlights
- Thin upper highlight line
- Very soft inner glow
- Slightly brighter top edge
- Restrained reflected light when appropriate

### Role
- Suggest light interaction
- Make surfaces feel real
- Remove dead digital flatness

### Avoid
- Obvious highlights
- Plastic shine
- Fake glossy effects
- Bright reflections with no logic

---

## Corner Radius

### Rules
- Larger radii for premium digital surfaces (6px-12px standard)
- Consistent across related objects
- Vary only with purpose
- Larger containers support more generous curvature
- Smaller details should not have cartoon radii

### Effect
- Softens interface
- Creates visual calm
- Makes panels feel designed
- Communicates modernity

---

## Grain & Texture

### When to Use
- Unifies surfaces
- Removes sterility
- Adds tactile character
- Extremely subtle (1-3% opacity)
- Even or semi-even distribution

### Avoid
- Muddying the design
- Damaging sharpness
- Gimmicky application
- Reducing premium feel

---

## Accent Color Behavior

### Use Accent For
- Key status points
- Important numbers
- Active states
- Select labels
- One strong surface if focal point needed

### Rules
- Limited distribution
- Creates hierarchy, not noise
- Less accent than you think
- Bright accent surrounded by restraint

### Avoid
- Repeating everywhere
- Full surface saturation
- Multiple competing intensities
- Low-priority details

---

## Contrast Control

### Use Contrast For
- Focal emphasis
- Surface separation
- Premium clarity
- Accent restraint

### Good Strategy
- Strong dark vs warm light base
- Accent used sparingly
- Micro-contrast inside surfaces
- Softened transitions where possible

### Avoid
- Everything high contrast
- Too many clashes
- Aggressive accent bursts
- Contrast as decoration

---

## Layer System

### Good Surface Layers
1. Background layer (subtle gradient or tint)
2. Primary panel layer (main content surface)
3. Secondary panel layer (supporting surfaces)
4. Accent layer (focal objects)
5. Light interaction layer (highlights, glows)

### Rules
- Each layer part of one material world
- Not every layer needs equal contrast
- Depth through tone and finish, not just color difference
- Lighter surfaces need softness
- Darker surfaces need subtle internal light

### Avoid
- Every card looking identical
- All surfaces equally important
- No distinction between primary/secondary
- Flat UI-card repetition

---

## Cohesion & Unified Design

### Cohesion Comes From
- Shared tonal logic
- Shared light behavior
- Shared radius system
- Shared shadow language
- Shared border behavior
- Shared accent behavior

### Avoid
- One section cinematic, another plain
- Inconsistent material language
- Random temperature shifts
- Disconnected effect styles

---

## Premium Quality Checklist

Before finalizing, verify:
- ✓ Surfaces feel shaped, not filled
- ✓ Shadows separate, don't just decorate
- ✓ Borders subtle enough
- ✓ Accent rare enough
- ✓ Background supports rather than performs
- ✓ Feels expensive without obvious effort
- ✓ No default, loud, or templated effects
- ✓ Emotionally premium before technically noticeable

---

## What to Remove Completely

Never use:
- Flat color blocks everywhere
- Hard outlines
- Generic SaaS cards
- Loud gradient branding
- Obvious glassmorphism
- Fake glossy highlights
- Heavy grain
- Default drop shadows
- Too many accents
- Overexposed white
- Lifeless black
- Decorative effects without function

---

## Final Rule

**Less, but better**
**Quieter, but stronger**
**Simpler, but more intentional**
**Calmer, but more expensive**

The best premium finish is noticed emotionally before technically.
