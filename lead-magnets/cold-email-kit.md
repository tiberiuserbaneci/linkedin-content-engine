## The Rules (Before You Use These)

These are not violations of common sense. They're the quality gate the outreach module checks before every email leaves the queue.

1. Line 1 must be specific about THEM — not "I came across your company" but "Your onboarding flow at [company.com] handles 3 steps manually that most teams automate"
2. Subject line under 6 words. No "Introduction" or "Quick question"
3. Under 150 words total. Cut ruthlessly.
4. Exactly 1 CTA. Not "let me know your thoughts" — a specific ask.
5. Sign-off is your name. That's it.
6. 95% of the email is about them. 5% is about you.

## The Scoring Note (Important)

These templates only work at full effectiveness when the lead was scored 8+ before the email was written.

A score of 8+ means:
- The company matches your ICP (size, industry, stage, tech stack)
- There's a real signal of need (recent post about the problem, job posting for the role you automate, news showing they're in the relevant stage)
- The contact is a decision-maker or champion
- They're not already a customer or active prospect

If you send these to a cold, unscored list, you'll get generic results from non-generic templates. Score first. Write second.
