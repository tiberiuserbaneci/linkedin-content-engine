## PART 3: THE 5-AGENT SYSTEM

### CORTEX — Reporting Automation
**Layer 5: Executive Intelligence**

**What it does:**
- Pulls business metrics (revenue, MRR, churn, pipeline, headcount)
- Generates executive summaries
- Creates dashboards and reports
- Highlights trends and anomalies

**Saves:** ~3 hours/week on Monday reporting  
**Use case:** CEO Monday update, board meetings, investor updates

**Input:** Business metrics, KPIs, time period  
**Output:** Formatted report, dashboard snapshot, key insights

---

### SPECTER — Lead Intelligence
**Layer 4: Prospect Enrichment**

**What it does:**
- Takes company name or founder LinkedIn
- Extracts: funding history, team size, tech stack, funding status

- Finds decision-makers and their roles

**Saves:** ~2 hours research per sales call  
**Use case:** Pre-call research, lead qualification, personalized outreach

**Input:** Company name, founder LinkedIn URL, industry signals  
**Output:** Prospect profile, vulnerabilities, decision-maker list, outreach angle

---

### STRIKER — Content Creation
**Layer 3: First Draft Generation**

**Saves:** Time on first draft (1-2 hours per piece)  
**Use case:** Email campaigns, LinkedIn posts, sales copy, company announcements

**Input:** Topic, context, tone, audience  
**Output:** First draft ready for editing

## PART 4: LINKEDIN CONTENT STRATEGY

### Why Ultron Creates Content
1. **Distribution** — Reach your ICP where they are (LinkedIn)
2. **Proof of concept** — Show Ultron works with real examples
3. **Lead gen** — Convert engaged viewers to users
4. **Authority** — Position Ultron as founder-focused AI tool

### 360Brew Algorithm
LinkedIn's 150B parameter model ranks content on:

**Highest ranking signals:**
- **Saves = 4-6x multiplier** (algorithm gold)
- Comments (2x multiplier)
- Reposts (1.5x multiplier)
- Dwell time (time spent reading)
- Click-through (profile visits, link clicks)

**What LinkedIn penalizes:**
- Hashtags (no hashtags = better performance)
- Engagement pods (looks fake)
- External links (stay on LinkedIn)
- Sales-y language (algorithm suppresses)
- Emoji spam (looks desperate)

### Best Posting Window
**Global:** 11:00–13:00 GMT (any day of week)  
**Romania:** 13:00–15:00 RO time  
**Avoid:** 03:49, 04:35, 08:42 (attracted wrong audience in testing)

### Hook Formulas (5 Types)

**Type 1: Contrarian Take**
```
"Most founders think [wrong belief].
Actually, [truth]."
```
Example:
```
"Most founders think AI should write for them.
Actually, AI should think for them."
```

**Type 2: Data Lead**
```
"[Number]% of [group] [stat/finding].
Here's why:"
```
Example:
```
"47% of teams use Claude to automate reporting.
Here's why it matters:"
```

**Type 3: Specific Situation**
```
"When you're [situation], [problem] is inevitable.
Here's the fix:"
```
Example:
```
"When you're scaling from 3 to 10 people, context loss is inevitable.
Here's the fix:"
```

**Type 4: Reframe**
```
"[Common advice] is wrong.
[Better advice] is the real win."
```
Example:
```
"'Automate everything' is wrong.
'Automate context management' is the real win."
```

**Type 5: Vulnerability + Lesson**
```
"I made [mistake].
Now I know [lesson]."
```
Example:
```
"I lost 2 hours weekly to manual reporting.
Now Ultron does it in 30 seconds."
```

### Post Structure
```
HOOK (2 lines exactly)
← Stops scroll, creates curiosity

BODY (14+ paragraphs, 10-19 words each)
← Active voice, one idea per paragraph
← Real examples, specific details
← No fluff, no "moreover," no "delve"

END (Repost or save prompt)
← "Repost if..." OR "Save this..."
← Creates engagement signal

CTA (First comment ONLY, never in body)
← Format: "Ultron [benefit] → [URL]"
← Routes to specific outcome
```

### Post Character Target
**1,242–2,500 characters** (optimal for LinkedIn algorithm)

### Content Types That Work
- ✅ Operational lessons (how we do X)
- ✅ Contrarian takes (why X is wrong)
- ✅ Data-backed insights (47% of teams...)
- ✅ Founder pain points (solved by Ultron)
- ✅ Before/after stories (manual → automated)

### What NOT to Do
- ❌ "AI is changing everything" (too generic)
- ❌ "Top 5 tips for..." (low save rate)
- ❌ Asking for engagement ("Like this if...")
- ❌ Competitor bashing (LinkedIn suppresses)
- ❌ Overly promotional (algorithm kills it)
- ❌ Multiple topics in one post (confuses algorithm)

### Reply Engagement Rules
- Always reply to first 20 comments
- Reply within 1 hour (prioritizes notification)
- Add value in replies (mini-insights, not "thanks!")
- Never ignore questions

## PART 7: CONCEPT SCORING FRAMEWORK (360BREW)

### Why Score Everything
- **Target: 85+** (build and post)
- **60-70:** Caution (redesign)
- **Below 60:** Rebuild (new concept)

### 4 Dimensions (25 pts each)

### Dimension 1: VIRALITY (Scroll-Stop Potential)
- **ScrollStop** (25%) — Does line 1 stop the scroll?
- **Clarity3s** (20%) — Understood in 3 seconds?
- **Novelty** (20%) — New angle or rehashed?
- **EmotionalTrigger** (20%) — Fear/FOMO/relief/pride?
- **FormatFit** (15%) — Right format for content?

**Scoring guide:**
- 0-30: Weak, common, no hook
- 30-60: Average, familiar angle
- 60-80: Strong, novel, clear hook
- 80-100: Exceptional, viral potential

### Dimension 2: SAVE-WORTHY (Algorithm Gold)
- **UtilityDensity** (25%) — Actionable per cm²?
- **Reusability** (25%) — Will they return to this?
- **Compression** (20%) — Max value, min words?
- **StructureClarity** (15%) — Skimmable?
- **Actionability** (15%) — Can use today?

**Scoring guide:**
- 0-30: Generic, low reusability
- 30-60: Some value, reference material
- 60-80: Practical, referenceable, actionable
- 80-100: High-value, frameworks, templates

### Dimension 3: VISUAL EFFICIENCY
- **HierarchyStrength** (25%) — Eye path obvious?
- **ContrastControl** (20%) — Dark/light works?
- **SpacingBalance** (20%) — Breathing room?
- **Consistency** (20%) — Colors, fonts systematic?
- **NoiseLevel** (15%) — Nothing competes for attention?

**Scoring guide:**
- 0-30: Cluttered, poor hierarchy
- 30-60: Acceptable, some hierarchy
- 60-80: Clean, professional, clear hierarchy
- 80-100: Premium, effortless hierarchy

### Dimension 4: SYSTEM THINKING (Authority)
- **FrameworkClarity** (30%) — Teachable in one image?
- **Modularity** (25%) — Components independent?
- **Transferability** (25%) — Reader can apply?
- **DepthVsSimplicity** (20%) — Deep but simple?

**Scoring guide:**
- 0-30: Ad-hoc, no system
- 30-60: Some structure, basic framework
- 60-80: Clear framework, transferable
- 80-100: Elegant, deep+simple, teachable

### Scoring Rules
- ✅ Score BEFORE spending time on design
- ✅ Be honest (don't inflate scores)
- ✅ If unsure, score lower
- ✅ Never post below 70

## PART 8: LAYOUT SELECTION SYSTEM

### Before Choosing Layout, Ask:
1. What's the primary message?
2. How many ideas? (1, 3, 5+)
3. Does it need data/comparison?
4. How much explanation needed?
5. Should it feel editorial or tactical?

### 10 Layout Types
1. **HERO_GRID** — One big headline, grid of supporting ideas
2. **TIMELINE** — Chronological progression
3. **COMPARISON** — Side-by-side analysis
4. **STEPS** — How-to, numbered progression
5. **ACROSTIC** — Vertical list with icons
6. **DATA_VISUAL** — One stat, heavy design
7. **QUOTE_HEAVY** — Testimonial or founder quote
8. **STORY_ARC** — Problem → Struggle → Solution
9. **FRAMEWORK** — Mental model, system
10. **MANIFESTO** — Beliefs, principles, contrarian takes

### 3 Master Templates
**Template 1: Light Editorial**
- Cream background (#F5ECE3)
- Dark text (#111111)
- Book Cloth accents (#CC785C)
- Substack-style, text-heavy
- Best for: Thought leadership, frameworks

**Template 2: Dark Hero + Light Body**
- Dark hero (Slate Dark #191919)
- Light body sections (Ivory Light #F5F5F0)
- Orange accents (#C94A22)
- Visual hierarchy strong
- Best for: Product stories, data-heavy

**Template 3: Structured Mixed**
- Cards, sections, clear separation
- Multiple background colors
- Strong typography hierarchy
- Icons, badges, visual markers
- Best for: How-tos, comparisons, multi-part stories

## PART 9: OPERATIONAL WORKFLOWS

### Founder Content Creation Workflow
1. **Brainstorm** (10 min) — What's the core idea?
2. **Score it** (5 min) — 360Brew score (target 85+)
3. **Outline** (10 min) — Hook + 5 body points
4. **Draft** (20 min) — Full copy with footer
5. **Design** (30 min) — Choose layout, create visual
6. **Export** (5 min) — PNG @ 1080×1350 @ 144dpi
7. **Preview** (5 min) — Check on phone, tablet, desktop
8. **Post** (2 min) — LinkedIn post + first comment CTA
9. **Monitor** (5 min/day) — Reply to comments, track metrics

**Total time: ~90 minutes per post**

### Quick Content Routing
- **Operational lesson** → Light Editorial template
- **Contrarian take** → Dark Hero template
- **Data-backed** → Data Visual layout
